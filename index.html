Project Proposal

Problem/Data Domain

Wildfires are becoming a bigger problem around the world, with extreme fire events increasing by 5.4% per year 2001, according to the World Resources Institute. In places like California, wildfires have caused massive destruction. For example, the recent Los Angeles fires caused over $350 million in damage to public infrastructure, including roads, utilities, and water systems. These fires also displaced families, destroyed homes, and led to poor air quality that impacted millions of residents. Climate change has made fire seasons longer and fires stronger, leading to more than 345 million hectares of land burning every year. These fires harm communities, damage nature, and make air quality worse. However, people often don’t have quick, easy access to important information, like where fires are happening or where to evacuate. This lack of real-time information makes it harder for communities to stay safe and prepared.

Some apps, like Watch Duty and AlertWildfire, try to help by tracking wildfires, but they have limits. They only cover certain areas, focus mostly on official updates, and don’t combine community reports with scientific data. These gaps leave people without the timely and reliable information they need to make quick decisions in emergencies. Our app will solve this problem by providing a more complete and accessible way for people to stay informed and prepared during wildfires.

App Description

The primary users of the application will be those who live in wildfire-prone areas, outdoor enthusiasts, business owners, and those who might be concerned about other family members. This app can provide accessibility to minority communities that are often underserved in high-risk wildfire areas.

Our app will use the OpenAI API to source recent news on wildfires which we will format to display on a user's newsfeed on the homepage. In addition, users will be able to write their own blog that users can view and share. Another feature we plan on adding is users will be able to to add their address to the site to see how at risk of a wildfire they are or share where wildfires could be. We also do not want to just let users know where wildfires are, but also be able to help them, which is why we are also giving users the ability to search and filter for fundraising events in their local area or other areas as well.

We will be using OpenAI's API along with Google Maps API. This will be paired with wildfire data from Kaggle. We will be starting with the California Wildfire data. For other wildfire data, users will be able to post wildfires in their area.

Our app will provide a practical, accessible, and reliable solution to the challenges faced with real-time wildfire awareness and response. Communities in wildfire prone areas often face challenges in accessing real-time updates, assessing their own personal risk, and finding resources for support and recovery. By having a personalized news feed and a community that is willing to contribute, users will always be up to date on wildfires in their area. The app will help users stay safe, connect with their community, and contribute to recovery efforts.
