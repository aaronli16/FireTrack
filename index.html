Project Proposal

Problem/Data Domain

Groceries being too expensive is a growing concern for many individuals and families trying to save money while making sustainable choices. Grocery prices have risen significantly over the past few years, with food prices up 28% since 2019, according to NerdWallet. Grocery prices vary across stores, and finding the best deals often requires shoppers to visit multiple stores or spend time researching prices online, one store at a time. Additionally, customers who want to purchase eco-friendly products face challenges in identifying sustainable options, often needing to research each product or brand individually. According to a McKinsey and NielsenIQ study, 78% of US consumers say a sustainable lifestyle is important to them, and 60% are willing to pay more for sustainable packaging. This demonstrates a clear need for an efficient solution that combines affordability and sustainability.

Apps such as Flipp try to address this problem by offering coupons and online ordering. However, they don't explicitly compare prices across a user's local grocery stores and lack features to highlight eco-friendly products. By creating this web app, we aim to bridge this gap, providing a tool that allows users to compare grocery prices across stores while also showcasing sustainable product options. Our app is designed to make grocery shopping an easier, more informed, and stress-free experience, empowering users to save money and make choices that align with their values.
